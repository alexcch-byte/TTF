Origins & Philosophy

"The art of Tang Soo Do Moo Duk Kwan demands mastery of the whole body—not merely kicking and punching. Technical skill without strong character is a burden on society. We practise to demonstrate virtue in action."

⚔ Historical Overview

Founded by Grandmaster Hwang Kee on 9 November 1945, Tang Soo Do Moo Duk Kwan draws its roots from Korea's indigenous fighting arts (Soo Bahk Ki and Tae Kyun), combined with principles from Northern and Southern Chinese Kung Fu, and the philosophical teachings of Do (Tao), No Ja (Lao Tzu), and Lee Do Ja (Confucius).

Grandmaster Hwang Kee first encountered martial arts at the age of seven, witnessing a lone man successfully defend himself against a group of attackers using only his feet. By 1936, he trained with Chinese Kung Fu master Yang Kuk Jin.

What is Tang Soo Do Moo Duk Kwan?

Tang Soo Do translates as "Hand Striking Way". Moo Duk Kwan translates as "Institute of Martial Virtue". Soo Bahk Ki (combat with bare hands and feet) is the oldest Korean martial art, approximately 2,000 years old. What separates it is its versatility and philosophy.

Sparring & Self-Defence

Comprehensive guide to One-Step Sparring (Ill Soo Sik Deh Ryun) and Ho Sin Sool (Self-Defence grabs).

Ho Sin Sool (Self-Defence Grabs)

Rotation 1 — Environmental Grabs

Hair Grab (Front) Step back into back stance. Grab attacker's hand covering hair, snapping it to hip to break grip. Cover position: back fist to temple (horse stance), reverse punch to solar plexus (front stance).
Belt Grab (Front) Step back into back stance, escape the grip by snapping hand to hip. Cover position: back fist to temple (horse stance), reverse punch to solar plexus (front stance).
Tackle (Front, Under Arms) Step back with arms wide to deflect and control tackle. Cover position: back fist to temple (horse stance), reverse punch to solar plexus (front stance).

Cross Hand Grabs (Rotations 2-3)

  • Grab 1 Escape side stance over arm. Knife hand (neck), Palm heel (chin, front stance), Spear hand (groin).
  • Grab 2 Over-hand wrist grip to chest. Escape and apply elbow pressure. Knife hand (neck), Palm heel (jaw line, swap front stance direction), Spear hand (groin).
  • Grab 3 Rotate under arm 180°. Wrist lock/break (palm down). Head butt (face).
  • Grab 4 Rotate wrist (thumb pressure point). Knife hand to back of wrist moving forward. Elbow attack (temple).

Same Side Grabs (Rotations 4-5)

  • Grab 1 Escape to side stance. Back fist (temple), Hammer fist (groin, horse stance), Ridge hand (jaw line, front stance).
  • Grab 2 Rotate hand, catch attacker's hand moving forward. Knife hand (neck). Knee attack (solar plexus) pulling attacker forward.
  • Grab 3 Move under arm rotating 180°. Wrist lock/break. Punch (temple). Front kick (rib) or roundhouse (solar plexus).
  • Grab 4 Rotate wrist, step back. Wrist lock (middle finger knuckle). Front kick (temple). Elbow strike (back of neck).

Shoulder, Bear Hugs & Lapel Grabs (Rotations 3-5)

Shoulder Grab (Side) Rotate arm counter-clockwise, upward arm bar/elbow pressure. Punch to face.

Double Shoulder Grab Step back, double upward block. Lift front leg, double knuckle strike to pectorals in front stance.
Bear Hug (Behind) Shift side, groin knife hand. Drop to horse stance, free arms. Double rib elbow. Reverse middle punch.

Bear Hug (Front) Grab attacker body, knee to groin. Drop to horse stance, double elbow strike. Reverse middle punch.
Single Lapel Grab Grab attacker wrist, pressure elbow. Arm bar stepping across into front stance. Elbow strike to their elbow.

Double Lapel Grab Grab one over, one under. Twist 45° across body into horse stance. Trap arms, inward elbow to head.

One-Step Sparring (Ill Soo Sik Deh Ryun)

10th-8th Gup One-Steps

  1. Simultaneous knife hand defence and front punch.
  2. Left hand reverse punch, simultaneous knife hand defence and front punch.
  3. High front snap kick - reverse middle punch - high front punch - step back.
  4. High turning side kick - reverse middle punch - high front punch - step back.

Rotation 1 (Pyung Ahn Oh Dan Level)

  1. Palm heel defence, knife hand to neck, spinning knife hand to neck.
  2. Low X-block against front kick, grab leg, reverse punch to centre.
  3. Step back right foot, double roundhouse kick (middle ball / high instep).
  4. Step left, reverse knife hand defence, grab shoulders, low side snap kick behind right knee.

Rotation 2 (Pyung Ahn Cho Dan)

  1. Step back left foot, palm heel down (45°) defence, knife hand to neck.
  2. Step back left foot, reverse knife hand defence, centre punch and high punch in horse stance.
  3. Step to left, middle side kick, knife hand to neck, step back.
  4. Step to left, middle roundhouse kick, knife hand to neck, reverse punch to solar plexus.

Rotation 3 (Pyung Ahn Sa Dan)

  1. Step back, knife hand defence, collar grab, knee attack to body.
  2. Step back, knife hand defence, head grab, knee attack to head.
  3. Pivot left 45° front stance, reverse knife hand defence, centre side snap kick into armpit, step back.
  4. Step back right foot, outside/inside kick defence, side kick to head.

Rotation 4 (Pyung Ahn E Dan)

  1. Simultaneous knife hand defence and attack to temple.
  2. Step back, knife hand defence, grab, step in with elbow attack.
  3. Step back right foot, turning side kick to body and head.
  4. Knife hand defence, round kick, right back-fist to temple, spinning elbow to solar plexus.

Rotation 5 (Pyung Ahn Sam Dan)

  1. Palm heel block, elbow to body, hammer fist to groin, back fist to bridge of nose.
  2. Knife hand defence, elbow lock.
  3. Twist, knife hand defence, hook kick to head, back fist to temple, pivot, spinning elbow to solar plexus.
  4. Outside-to-inside kick (partner retreats), spinning back hook kick.

3rd–2nd Gup One-Steps

Attacker: Low defence then stepping high punch (right foot back).

Tech 1: Step 45° inward right into back stance. Simultaneous left knife hand block and right high punch moving into horse stance. Step back left leg to fighting stance. High front snap kick right (front) leg — reverse middle punch — high punch. Land right leg forward in front stance.

Tech 2: Step 45° inward right into back stance. Left hand middle punch, chamber both hands to right hip. Simultaneous left knife hand block and right high punch moving into horse stance. Step back right leg to fighting stance. High turning side kick right leg — reverse middle punch — high punch. Land right leg forward in front stance.

Tech 3: Step back left leg into back stance, outside/inside palm block with right arm. Cross arms, inward right elbow strike to middle stepping forward into horse stance. Turn counter-clockwise, feet together. High back kick with left leg — reverse middle punch right — high punch left. Land in front stance.

Tech 4: Step back left leg into fighting stance, outside/inside palm block with right arm. Cross arms, inward right elbow strike to middle stepping forward into horse stance. Back turning left elbow strike. Turn clockwise back to fighting stance, right leg back. High roundhouse kick right leg — reverse middle punch left — outside/inside high knife hand right. Land in front stance.

Advanced: 1st Gup to 1st Dan

Attacker: Low defence then stepping high punch. Odd techniques = right foot back; Even = left foot back (mirror).

Tech 1 (Right back): 45° inside/outside knife block left — twisting right mid punch — twisting left high punch — grab outstretched hand — high right roundhouse kick.

Tech 2 (Left back): 45° inside/outside knife block right — twisting left mid punch — twisting right high punch — grab outstretched hand — high left roundhouse kick.

Tech 3 (Right back): Outside-inside block right — twisting left mid punch — twisting right high punch — step left behind right — high right side kick.

Tech 4 (Left back): Outside-inside block left — twisting right mid punch — twisting left high punch — step right behind left — high left side kick.

Tech 5 (Right back): Right mid front kick — step forward front stance O/I block right — reverse mid punch left — hold arm — inside-to-outside face kick with left leg.

Tech 6 (Left back): Left mid front kick — step forward front stance O/I block left — reverse mid punch right — hold arm — inside-to-outside face kick with right leg.

Tech 7 (Right back, arm bent): Left mid front kick — step back to horse stance — step front back leg right roundhouse — without placing foot deliver spinning back inside-outside kick to arm — reverse high punch left to temple.

Tech 8 (Left back, arm bent): Right mid front kick — step back to horse stance — step front back leg left roundhouse — without placing foot deliver spinning back inside-outside kick to arm — reverse high punch right to temple.

Free Sparring (Jayu Dae Ryun)

Dae Ryun means "sparring". Free sparring (Jayu Dae Ryun) is the living application of Tang Soo Do — controlled, full-speed exchange against a resisting opponent. It develops distance (Ma Kan), timing (Wan Gup), and composure under pressure, while character and control are valued above winning.

Three Levels of Sparring Practice

Step 1

Sam Soo Sik Dae Ryun

Three-step sparring. Pre-arranged attack and defence over three steps. Builds basic distance, blocking and counter-attack with no surprise — the foundation for all sparring.

Step 2

Il Soo Sik Dae Ryun

One-step sparring. A single committed attack met with an immediate, decisive combination. Trains explosive timing and precise targeting (see the Sparring & Self-Defence section for the full syllabus).

Step 3

Jayu Dae Ryun

Free sparring. Both partners move and attack freely with controlled contact. The unscripted test of everything — stances, kicks, hand techniques, breathing and spirit (Ki Hap).

Legal Target Areas

  • Front and sides of the torso (above the belt)
  • Front and sides of the head (controlled contact)
  • Solar plexus, ribs and abdomen

Prohibited Techniques

  • Strikes to the back, spine, neck or throat
  • Attacks to the groin, knees or joints
  • Uncontrolled / excessive contact
  • Open-hand strikes to the face, head butts, takedowns

Sparring Etiquette & Spirit

  • Bow to the flag, the referee and your opponent before and after every match.
  • Control is mastery — a technique landed with excessive force concedes the point.
  • Maintain composure (Chung Shin Tong Il); never spar in anger.
  • Protect your partner: sparring is shared practice, not a fight.
